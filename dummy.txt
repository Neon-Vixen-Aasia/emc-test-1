THE WAY I APPROACH SELF-LEARNING :
I have realized that I am not someone who studies every day or tries to learn everything at once. Instead, I prefer focusing on the skills that truly matter for my career and giving them my complete attention when the time is right.
Yesterday, I completed a Git and GitHub tutorial as part of my self-learning journey. Whenever I learn something new, I maintain two separate notebooks. One is dedicated to study notes, where I write down concepts and important points. The other is a task checklist, where I track my progress and record what I have completed and understood.
I do not write notes with the intention of memorizing them repeatedly. I write them so that I can revisit and recall the concepts whenever I need them in the future. Similarly, my checklist serves as a reminder of how far I have come and gives me confidence in my progress.
Over time, I have learned that effective learning is not necessarily about studying every single day. It is about having clarity, focus, and the ability to dedicate uninterrupted time to a particular skill. When you know where you want to go and why you are learning something, the process becomes much more meaningful.
As aspiring professionals and developers, we do not need to chase every skill at once. We need a clear goal, focused effort, and the discipline to keep moving forward.
With clarity and concentration, learning any skill becomes possible.
